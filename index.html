Nina Simone

The High Priestess of Soul

Biography

Nina was an American singer, songwriter, musician and civil rights activist. To make a living, she started playing the piano at a nightclub in Atlantic city. Where she was told that she had to sing, which started her career as a jazz vocalist. Nina, had prepared an audition for the Curtis Institute of Music but this application, was denied. She always thought that her application was denied due to a racial prejudice.

Civil Rights era

Nina always included songs in her repertoire that drew on her African-American heritage. Then, as a response to the murder of Medgar Evers, she wrote Mississippi Goddam. This became one of the many protest songs written by Nina. They song was boycotted in some southern states.

She's is not only recognised for her music, but also for her activism in the racial discourse in America.
